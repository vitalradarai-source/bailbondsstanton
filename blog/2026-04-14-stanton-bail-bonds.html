Where Stanton Arrests Are Processed

Someone arrested in Stanton is transported to the OCSD West Region substation serving Stanton at 11100 Cedar Street, Stanton, CA 90680 (phone: 714-647-7000). Stanton is an Orange County Sheriff's Department contract city, which means there is no standalone city jail. Arrests in Stanton are handled by OCSD deputies and booked directly at the Orange County Intake and Release Center.

For serious felony charges or when the city facility is at capacity, defendants transfer to the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC) at 550 N Flower Street, Santa Ana, CA 92703. This is the intake point for the Orange County Sheriff's Department (OCSD) jail system, which also includes the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange and the James A. Musick Facility in Irvine. Stanton in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

Stanton is a West Orange County contract city — policing is provided by the Orange County Sheriff's Department, not a Stanton PD. OCSD deputies work out of the West Operations substation serving Stanton, Buena Park, and portions of nearby cities. There is no Stanton city jail; arrests book directly into the OCSD system through the Intake and Release Center in Santa Ana, with most inmates held at Theo Lacy.

The city sits at the intersection of the 22 freeway and Beach Boulevard, with Knott's Berry Farm immediately north in Buena Park and Garden Grove to the south. This corridor — Beach Boulevard, the 22, and the surrounding commercial strips — generates consistent arrest volume. DUI stops on the 22 between Knott and Valley View, public intoxication calls from the Beach Boulevard motel corridor, and retail theft from the strip-mall retailers are the three most common categories.

Because Stanton has no city jail, families calling around trying to locate an arrested relative need to route through the OCSD inmate locator rather than a city PD — and that locator runs 2–6 hours behind actual booking. How a Stanton Bail Bond Actually Works

You call (626) 478-1062. We confirm the bail amount from the Orange County bail schedule — stanton bail bonds typically range from $5,000 for petty theft (PC 484) to $50,000 for domestic violence (PC 273.5) and $100,000 for DUI with injury (VC 23153). You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — the state-set rate under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. We post the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond. The defendant is released, typically 1–4 hours at OCSD West Patrol (Stanton) and 4–8 hours at the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC).

The 10% is a fee for services — it is a premium paid to the bondsman, not a loan. When the case concludes and all court appearances are made, the bond is exonerated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a Stanton arrest actually get processed?

Stanton is an OCSD contract city — no Stanton PD, no city jail. Deputies book arrestees directly into the Orange County system via the IRC at 550 N Flower Street, Santa Ana. Most defendants are held at Theo Lacy Facility in Orange pending bail.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Stanton?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Stanton bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Once the booking process is complete and the Orange County bail schedule amount is assigned, a licensed bondsman can post the bond immediately. Most Stanton DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
